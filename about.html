01 — Our Story

Built to fix a problem we kept seeing.

Too many good businesses were being represented by weak websites — slow, generic, and disconnected from how they actually operate.

Lumostat started with a simple observation: most agencies were shipping templates dressed up as custom work, and most in-house teams didn't have the bandwidth to do design justice. Businesses were left choosing between "fast and generic" or "thoughtful and slow." We didn't think that trade-off was necessary.

So we built a studio around a narrower promise — every project gets senior attention, from strategy through launch. No account handoffs, no junior teams learning on a client's budget. The same people who plan the work also design and build it.

That discipline is still the whole model. We work with a limited number of clients at a time, on purpose, so the craft doesn't slip as we grow. It's slower to scale, but it's the only way we've found to keep the work honest.

04 — How We Work

A process built for clarity, not ceremony.

Four stages, the same for every engagement. Each one ends with something concrete you can react to.

01
Discover
We start by understanding the business — goals, audience, competitors, and what "success" actually looks like. No design work begins until this is settled.
02
Plan
Sitemap, content strategy, and technical approach get locked in writing. This is where scope, timeline, and budget become concrete commitments.
03
Design
We design in the browser, not just in mockups, so what a client approves is close to what actually ships. Feedback rounds are structured and fast.
04
Launch
QA across devices, performance and accessibility checks, then a coordinated go-live. We stay on for support after the site is public.

07 — Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we hear before a project starts.

We follow the four-stage process outlined above: Discover, Plan, Design, Launch. You'll always know which stage a project is in and what's due next — we send a simple written plan at kickoff so nothing is ambiguous.
You'll have a shared channel with your project lead and weekly async updates. No layers of account managers relaying messages — you talk directly to the people building your site.
Most marketing sites take 4–6 weeks from kickoff to launch. Larger product builds run 8–14 weeks depending on scope. You'll get a firm timeline once we've scoped the work in the Plan stage.
Fixed project pricing, agreed before work begins — no hourly billing surprises. Pricing depends on scope and complexity; most engagements start at $8,000. We'll give you a firm number after the discovery call.
Two structured feedback rounds are built into the Design stage. In practice, most projects need fewer because we design in the browser and share progress early and often.
Every project includes a 30-day launch warranty covering fixes at no cost. Beyond that, we offer monthly support plans for updates, monitoring, and small feature additions.
